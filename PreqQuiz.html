Intramuros

The Walled City of Manila

Intramuros is one of the most memorable places for me in the Philippines. It holds historical significance and offers a quiet contrast to the busy atmosphere of the city.

Intramuros is located in Manila, along the southern bank of the Pasig River. It is surrounded by modern city areas, yet it remains distinct because of its stone walls and preserved architecture.

Intramuros was built during the Spanish colonial period in the late 16th century. It served as the political and military center of Spanish rule in the Philippines and was originally constructed to protect the city from foreign invasions.

This place is memorable to me because it is where my life in the city really started. It’s where I began high school, and back then, my friends and I would often walk around its streets on our way home, talking about school, life, and the little things that mattered to us. It’s also a place my family frequently visits whenever we just want to go out, have fun, or spend time together. Over the years, Intramuros has become more than just a historical landmark it holds pieces of my everyday life, the routines we followed, and the laughter we shared. Every corner of this place brings back a memory, whether it’s a quiet walk with friends, a family outing on weekends, or simply passing through after school. Its walls and streets are reminders of my beginnings, the moments that shaped my early teenage years, and the sense of comfort that comes from familiar places shared with the people I care about.

Intramuros isn’t just a historical site, it’s part of my personal story. Every visit reminds me of my early years, family moments, and the little routines that shaped my life in the city.
