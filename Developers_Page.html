Biography

It's nothing but a fleeting memory

Childhood Years

      Some would say that it was the happiest day of their lives while for some, it's nothing but a fleeting memory. While it is the happiest day for others, it's nothing but a distant memory now. Back then, we didn't care about the world. We only cared about ourselves, about the toys that we played with, and the joy that we spent with our friends. I don't remember much when I was a child but there is one thing that stuck out with me the most, that was when I got my first bicycle. I grew up with my grandmother back then because of my mothers work and when I finally got to meet her again I asked her to buy me a bicycle in which she did.

      Now here's the problem, I grew up at my mother's province where everything were pretty much just pebbles and sand on the road, there were no pavements or cement road to ride a bicycle on to. But I was just a child and I want nothing more than to have fun while I still can. That's why I rode it as fast as I can and ended up falling because the road was too bumpy to ride on. I still have my scar from that day but now it's pretty vague, most of my childhood is either erased from my memory or it's just another fleeting memory. Still, that bicycle reminds me that even painful moments can become something worth remembering.

Teenage Years

      If my childhood is nothing but a fleeting memory then my teens is just a blank page, I only remember bits of pieces about my life. During that year my mother took me back here in the city so that I can continue my studies and be finally with her after all this years. It was pretty scary back then, I don't know anyone here, all of my friends were back home and I was just here all by myself where the only person that I know in this place is my mom. My mother, just told me on how to go to my new school without helping me with traveling there at all.

     I have to traverse the big city all by myself, it was scary at first but I got used to it as time goes by. I joined the YMCA club when I was in 8th grade which is pretty fun, we went to a lot of schools and through that club I slowly learned how to socialize again. Being around new people helped me feel less alone in a place that once felt unfamiliar. Even though my memories are blurry, those moments taught me how to be independent. Looking back now, my teenage years may be a blank page, but they quietly shaped who I am today.

College Years

      As for my college years, everything felt heavier but clearer at the same time. I was no longer just trying to survive each day, I was trying to figure out who I really was and what I wanted to become. College taught me that freedom comes with responsibility, and that every choice I make has consequences I have to face on my own. There were days when deadlines piled up and motivation was hard to find, but there were also moments when I felt proud just for making it through. I met people who came and went, some who stayed for a while, and some who taught me lessons without even realizing it.

     Unlike my childhood and teenage years, college forced me to be present, even when I wanted to disappear. It wasnt always happy or exciting, but it was real. And maybe that is what makes my college years different they are not just memories fading away, theyre experiences that continue to shape me every day. Im still in the middle of this journey, learning from my mistakes as much as my small victories. Unlike before, I cant just drift through things anymore because my choices now actually matter. I dont know exactly where Im headed yet, but I know that these years are slowly shaping me. For now, my college life isnt a conclusion it's still a work in progress.
