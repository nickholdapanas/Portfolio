Biography

Childhood Years

Some would say that childhood is the happiest time of their lives, while for others it becomes nothing more than a fleeting memory. Back then, we didn’t care about the world only about ourselves, our toys, and the joy of being with friends.

I don’t remember much from my childhood, but one memory stands out clearly: getting my first bicycle. I grew up with my grandmother due to my mother’s work, and when I finally reunited with her, I asked for a bicycle which she gave me.

The roads where we lived were made of pebbles and sand. There were no proper pavements, but I rode anyway. I fell, leaving a scar that has since faded. Much like my memories, it’s still there just less visible.

Teenage Years

If my childhood is a fleeting memory, then my teenage years feel like a blank page. I only remember fragments. Moving to the city forced me to learn independence early.

Joining the YMCA club in eighth grade helped me reconnect with people and slowly adapt. Even if much of that time feels blurred, it shaped how I handle unfamiliar situations.

College Years

College feels heavier, but clearer. I’m no longer drifting every choice has weight now. These years aren’t fading memories; they’re ongoing experiences.

I don’t know exactly where I’m headed yet, but I know my choices now matter. My college life isn’t a conclusion it’s still a work in progress.
